    Bias is common but can have harmful effects, both for the people labeled with stereotypes and the ones doing the labeling.

    But let's focus on you.


    Stick to the facts.

    Are you a wizard? If the answer to that is no, you probably can't read other people's minds. Nor can you know for certain what intentions they may have or what circumstances they are experiencing.

    You may think the large man who followed you into the convenience store is about to rob the place, but it's more likely he came in to get a snack. Just like you did.


    Leave the judgments behind.

    This is a tough one. It's very easy to label things as good or bad, valuable or useless.

    But most things in life are neither good nor bad, they just are. This also applies to humans. Maybe you don't like the way your neighbor dresses. That's okay. However, the clothing she chooses to adorn herself with don't actually say anything about her character. They don't say anything at all because fabric cannot speak!


    Be mindful of your mind.

    Sometimes you might find yourself feeling uncomfortable or on edge around others. Take a moment and ask yourself why.

    Am I uncomfortable because this person looks different from me?
    Because they use different words than I do?
    Because they make choices I don't understand?

    Do those things actually, really, truly have an effect on my life?
    Am I trying to read minds and look into the future?
    Are my feelings based in reality, or from opinions I've heard?


    Find similarities and celebrate differences.

    People all over the planet are essentially the same. We laugh when things are funny, cry when we feel alone. We admire beautiful sunsets and dance to our favorite songs. We feel passionate and inspired watching amazing feats of athleticism. We love and fight with our whole hearts.

    But the things that make us different are also incredible! The myriad cultures make our world exciting. Learning from others can broaden our horizons, inspire new ideas, and make the world even better.
